✦ VIENNOISERIES ✦

Viennoiseries are a category of French baked goods that are made with a yeast-leavened dough, often enriched with butter, eggs, and milk, giving them a rich, flaky texture. They are typically sweeter and more delicate than bread but not as rich as pastries. The term comes from "Vienna" (Wien), as these treats were inspired by Austrian baking traditions and popularized in France.

✦ TARTES ✦

Tartes are French pastries consisting of a pastry crust filled with either sweet or savory ingredients. They can be open-faced or covered with a lattice or full crust. The base is typically made from pâte sucrée (sweet shortcrust pastry) or pâte brisée (buttery, flaky pastry).

✦ POUND CAKES ✦

Pound Cake is a rich, dense, and buttery cake that gets its name from its traditional recipe: equal parts (one pound each) of flour, butter, sugar, and eggs. This classic cake originated in Europe and has evolved into many variations over time.
