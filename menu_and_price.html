Viennoiseries

Viennoiseries are a category of French baked goods that are made with a yeast-leavened dough, often enriched with butter, eggs, and milk, giving them a rich, flaky texture. They are typically sweeter and more delicate than bread but not as rich as pastries. The term comes from "Vienna" (Wien), as these treats were inspired by Austrian baking traditions and popularized in France.
